Maintaining Creative Control
in Shifting Landscapes

Research Dashboard & Documentation

Research Question

How does the intentional use of design constraints and manual vectorization preserve human authorship when using AI-generated imagery as creative reference material?

Deep Dive Focus

Maintaining creative control, human authorship, and intentional design process while using AI as a reference tool in vector landscape creation.

Core Themes

human authorship intentionality creative control AI collaboration vector landscapes design constraints reflective design research

Project Evolution

Initial Direction

At the beginning of this project, the focus was on exploring how designers can co-create with AI when producing vector landscapes.

Research Shift

As research and experimentation progressed, the focus shifted away from using AI as a direct image generator and toward understanding how designers can maintain authorship and intentionality when AI is used only as reference material.

Current Focus

The project now explores how design constraints and manual vectorization allow designers to preserve creative control while still benefiting from AI-generated imagery as inspiration.

Insight

AI appears to be most valuable when it acts as a reference generator rather than a design author. By introducing intentional constraints and manually translating imagery into vector forms, the designer maintains authorship while still benefiting from AI-assisted exploration.
